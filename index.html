Most companies lose money on vehicles in three places.

If trucks and vans run your business, they are also one of your largest uncontrolled costs. The losses rarely show up as one bad deal. They leak out a few thousand dollars at a time, on every vehicle you buy, hold, and sell.

You sell too low

When it is time to move a truck, you take the easy exit: a trade-in, an auction, or a leasing-company buyback. You accept the first number offered, and that number is almost always thousands below what the vehicle is worth. On the Rottler fleet, that gap ran about $8,900 per truck, given up again on every unit they turned over.

You buy under pressure

A truck dies or a new hire starts Monday, and the clock is already running. So you buy whatever is on the lot, at whatever price the dealer names, with the wrong setup and a warranty nobody had time to read. Reactive buying is expensive buying, and your team is the one stuck driving the compromise.

You hold too long with no plan

With no replacement strategy, vehicles get kept until the repair bills, downtime, and roadside failures pile up. By the time you finally react, you have paid twice: once for repairs you should have avoided, and again in resale value that quietly disappeared while you waited.

Most owners never see the full number, because it never lands as one big bill. It leaves quietly, a few thousand dollars at a time, until it is gone for good.
