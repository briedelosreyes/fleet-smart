Most companies lose money on vehicles in three places.

The problem is rarely one bad deal. It is a weak system repeated across every purchase, sale, repair decision, and replacement cycle.

Selling too low

Trade-ins, auctions, and leasing-company buybacks are easy. They can also leave thousands of dollars behind on each truck or van.

Buying under pressure

When a vehicle goes down, owners rush. Rushed buying creates bad pricing, poor fit, weak warranty choices, and downtime.

Repair bills with no plan

One major transmission, engine, or electrical failure can hit cash flow hard. Two at once can wreck a month.
